Welcome to the Heavy Ions Physics & Electromagnetic Calorimeter Operations in CMS

A project hosted by the CBPF and supported by FAPERJ E-26/2021JPS-E_40/2021.

In this proposal we present plans to nucleate a new research line at CBPF in Rio de Janeiro, working in Heavy Ion Physics and in the Electromagnetic Calorimeter (ECAL) of the Compact Muon Solenoid (CMS) experiment at CERN. We pursue experimental analyses not yet explored within the CMS heavy-ion group, plus phenomenology studies of new physics in photon-induced processes with heavy-ion beams, while building human capacity that contributes to the future of science in Brazil.

Read the full project description…

In addition, we propose innovative work aimed at CMS detector operations: developments in the ECAL trigger system and performance verification through monitoring and calibration during the new LHC Run 3 data-taking period (started in 2022). This project encompasses cutting-edge topics at the frontier of contemporary physics enabled by ever more powerful accelerators. It is scientifically relevant within the High Energy Physics (HEP) community, of excellent quality, and employs innovative methodologies—areas not yet developed within ICTs in Rio de Janeiro.

We aim to open new, important research fronts at CBPF with multidisciplinary and interdisciplinary characteristics across research, teaching, and outreach. Major advances in our understanding of ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions are expected from upcoming LHC data, along with prospects at future accelerators—reinforcing our view that this is an outstanding, internationally competitive project. Support from FAPERJ is essential to elevate CBPF as a center of excellence in Heavy Ion Physics and CMS ECAL operations in Rio de Janeiro.

About the Coordinator

Patricia Rebello Teles holds a BSc in Mathematics from Universidade Federal do Rio de Janeiro (1991) and an MSc in Physics from the Instituto de Física da Universidade de São Paulo (2006), where she researched Nuclear Physics applied to Medicine (Proton Radiation Therapy) under Prof. Dr. Mahir Saleh Hussein.

She continued in the Mathematical Physics Department advised by Prof. Dr. Oscar Pinto Éboli. In February 2008 she moved to the Universidade Federal do ABC to complete her PhD in Particle Physics Phenomenology at the LHC under Dr. José Kenichi Mozukoshi.
